Design for Manufacturing

About Design for Manufacturing (DFM)

DFM is an approach that focuses on designing products so they can be manufactured more easily, cost-effectively, and efficiently. It includes guidelines and principles that aim to simplify the manufacturing process, reduce material costs, and improve product quality. DFM is often viewed as a standalone practice because it addresses specific challenges and objectives in manufacturing.

As per IATF 16949 Definition:

DFM - Design for Manufacturing is the integration of product development and process planning to design the product in such a way that it can be manufactured as simply and efficiently as possible.

Focus Areas:

  • Manufacturability analysis of processes, process steps, sub-assemblies
  • Clever, robust and beneficial design
  • Structured & adaptable Questionnaire
  • Define adaptations, modifications or further developments

How is DFM Used?

  • Analysis of the function and its individual components
  • Consideration of suitable manufacturing processes to produce the components in the best possible way
